# [[How to Write effective E-Mails]]

> You don't need to read the mail to understand it.

## Subject Line

Put the precise point in the subject line - not the topic, the **point**!

- ❌ Too generic: "Q1 Meeting"
- ✅ Precise: "Q1 Meeting Summary - 3 action items for you"

People work multiple projects and don't have time.
Be specific.
You are not a well sold ficiton author were people run in your door to read your stuff!

## Structure

1. Start with what you want.
2. Context and background go after.

- Use [[MECE Principles]] headings (Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive)
- Prefer bullet points. People scan, they don't read
- **Highlight names** so **Dana** knows instantly what concerns her

### Example layout

```
Focus initiatives:
- Spain: XXX
- Germany: XXX

Next steps:
- Clara: schedule appointment
- Frank: upload slides
```

## Push for Action

Don't ask open questions. Provide options and set deadlines.

- ✅ "I'm available 10am or 4pm for 30 min - does either work?"
- ✅ "Please confirm by Friday 3pm"
- ❌ "Let me know when you're free"
- ❌ "Please get back to me at your earliest convenience"

You save at least one back and forth that way.
Plus, people don't have to check your barely organized calendar

## When Not to Use Email

- **Angry?**: Sleep on it first.
- **Quick back-and-forth?**: Use Chats like Teams, Wire, Discord, etc. instead.
- **Bad news?**: Deliver it in person. Good news in writing — it's permanent and shareable.
