# [[Land Use & Solar Energy Potential (USA)]]

Solar power is NOT a "space problem."

If you'd use all the "corn for ethanol" land for solar you have more than double the electricity needed for the US!
If all the land used `oil production` was used for solar production instead, you could run almost the entire U.S. on solar alone.


#### Oil and Gas Production (Onshore)
- Active infrastructure oil & gas (well pads, access roads, processing facilities) occupies approx. 46.000 km² (roughly the size of Vermont and New Hampshire combined).[1]
- Total Leased Area: Including non-producing but reserved federal lands, the area totals approx. 96.000 km² (23.7 million acres).[2]
- Infrastructure: Over 1.1 million active wells and a pipeline network of approx. 4.8 million kilometers define the landscape.

#### Corn for Ethanol (Gas)
- The USA uses about 120.000km² (30 million acres) for corn for ethanol production.[4]
- Installed capacity: approx. 6.000 Gigawatts (GW) (based on 50MW/km²)
- Annual Yiel: approx. 9.000 Terrawatt hours (TWh) (based on 1.500 kWh/kW).

That's 225% of the U.S. electricity demand (Based: ~4.000 TWh/year).[3]
You can also electrify the entire states. Even in the most outlandish scenarios that need 50% additional electricity, you are vocered .[7]

#### Theoretical Solar Potential (Calculated Model)
If the 46.000 km² currently used for oil infrastructure were utilized for utility-scale solar PV:
- Installed Capacity: approx. 2.300 Gigawatts (GW) (based on 50 MW/km²). 
- Annual Yield: approx. 3.450 Terawatt-hours (TWh) (based on 1.500 kWh/kW).
Roughly 86% of total U.S. electricity demand (Base: ~4.000 TWh/year).[3]


## Notes

#### Power Density - 50MW/km² used
Used 50MW/km². Which is a ~30% safety margin to 2019 numbers for solar plants.
Fixed tilt solar parks have an AC/km² density factor of 70, while tracked plants get 44.5 MW AC/km².[6]

- Fixed tilt: 0.35MW DC/acre ~ 0.87 DC/hectar. 
- Tracking plant: 0.24MW DC/acre ~ 0.59MW DC/hectar

- Fixed tilt: 0.28 MW AC/acre ~ 0.69 MW AC/hectare[6]
- Tracking plant: 0.18 MW AC/acre ~ 0.45 MW AC/hectare

Fixed tilt: ~70MW AC/km²
Tracking plant: ~ 44.5 MW AC/km²

#### Yield per 1 kW of installed capacity per year (USA) - 2017 numbers
Used 1.500 kWh/kW. Conservative New Jersex numbers.[5]
- US National Average (approx. 24%): approx. 2.100 kWh/kW installed capacity.
- New Jersey (Conservative) approx. 17%: approx. 1.500 kWh/kW installed capacity

Formula: `1 kW x 8.760 hrs/year x capacity factor`

Regional Differences (EIA 2019 Examples)
The yield varies significantly by location:

- Arizona: ~26.3% Capacity Factor (approx. 2,300 kWh/kW)
- North Carolina: ~21% Capacity Factor (approx. 1,800 kWh/kW)
- New Jersey: ~16.8% Capacity Factor (approx. 1,300 kWh/kW)
- Massachusetts: 16.5% Capcaity Factor (approx. )
